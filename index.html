ABB FIA Formula E World Championship

is the pinnacle of electric motorsport. It represents the pinnacle of efficiency and innovation, where the world's best drivers compete for the championship title on tight, demanding city street circuits spanning the globe.

Unlike many other series, where races last for several hours, in Formula E teams face a high-speed strategic chess match of energy management within a strict 45-minute limit. This makes the championship not only a competition of speed but also a showcase of futuristic technology and sustainable mobility.

Basic principles of Formula E:

  • Distance:

    A race typically lasts around 45 minutes plus one lap (or a set number of laps), turning intense street circuits into a high-speed chess match of energy management.

  • Aerodynamics:

    Gen3 cars maximize efficiency over high downforce. They are uniquely engineered for tight city street tracks, using low-drag designs to minimize battery consumption while handling sharp walls and close overtaking.

  • Electric power:

    Top-tier cars use cutting-edge 350 kW electric motors combined with ultra-advanced regenerative braking systems, recapturing up to 40% of the energy used during the race directly from the brakes.
